# Diligence Workflow

## Sequence

1. Intake
   Inventory the dataroom, count files, infer categories, and identify obvious gaps.
2. Scoping
   Define the company thesis, financing stage, expected use of proceeds, and the main underwriting questions.
3. Specialist review
   Run domain reviews in parallel where possible. Keep each domain focused on its own evidence set.
4. Cross-check
   Compare claims across deck, financial model, KPI exports, customer references, contracts, and roadmap material.
5. Synthesis
   Convert specialist findings into one decision-ready view with explicit open items.

## Minimum Deliverables

- `Dataroom coverage`: what was reviewed, what was unreadable, what was absent
- `Risk register`: issue, severity, evidence, why it matters
- `Key questions`: management follow-ups that affect conviction
- `Recommendation`: present the current decision posture and what could change it

## Severity Guide

- `Critical`: can independently kill the deal or invalidate core assumptions
- `High`: materially weakens underwriting but may be remediable
- `Medium`: notable concern that needs follow-up
- `Low`: worth tracking but unlikely to move the decision on its own
